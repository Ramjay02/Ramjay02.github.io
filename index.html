History

Dipolog’s earliest recorded history started in 1834 when a civil government was organized by the Spanish Provincial government of Misamis, under whose jurisdiction we then belonged, with the appointment of a “Captain” as town executive, ‘Teniente’ and an ‘Aguacil’ to maintain law and order. Don Domingo Ruiz, a native, was the town executive that year when the town site transferred from Sitio Tulwanan to where it is now.

Tradition says that in that year, a Spanish Recollect Missionary arrived in Tulwanan believing that the townsite was still there. Upon meeting a native, asked: “Donde esta el Capitan?” (Where is the captain?) Our unknown hero, understanding only word ‘capitan’ pointed to the west and said in Subano “Di. . .pag,” meaning ‘across the river’. Guided by his ‘muchacho’, a Tagalog boy named Antonio Subido, the Padre proceeded downriver and upon reaching the townsite named the place “Dipag”. Through the years, this was corrupted by mispronunciation and intermingling of Visayan and Subano words into what it is today “Dipolog”.

Cathedral of Our Lady of the Most Holy Rosary

The Cathedral of Our Lady of the Most Holy Rosary is one of the favorite tourist destinations in Dipolog City because it is designed by our one and only national hero, Dr. Jose Rizal. It is said that the church was established sometime in the year 1895 and that Dr. Jose Rizal himself designed the altar.

Dipolog Sunset Boulevard

Who wouldn’t love to witness the captivating scenery of the setting sun, right? If Manila has the famous Roxas Boulevard, then Dipolog City has the Dipolog Sunset Boulevard! That’s right, and it is directly facing the beautiful Sulu Sea. Yep, you’ll get to enjoy that fresh and clean sea breeze while having a picnic with your special someone!

Sta. Cruz Marker

Located in Dipolog City is the oldest landmark of Christianity. It is also the oldest landmark of the city, situated on one of its oldest streets. It is said that the cross was erected to commemorate the time when Boholanos first landed in the city and called it their home. Hence, the beautiful city of Dipolog was born.

3003 Steps to Linabo Peak

From the peak you have a 360-degree view of the twin cities. I suggest you start your climb early morning, around 6 am on a weekend so by 9 am you would reach the top where telcos and national TV stations have their repeater antennas.Bring bottles of water and food since there are no restaurants there. A consolation is that there are stores at the foot and near the peak in the vicinity of the Lugdungan elementary school.But bring along your garbage after your picnic at the top.

Museo Dipolog

Beautiful building. Attended an art exhibit on the second floor. Ample space with clean washroom. There is an art workshop going on at the time of our visit. It is free to visit. Other than this, there's nothing much to see but it's worthwhile to check out the cathedral, city hall and plaza Magsaysay nearby.

Magsaysay Park

You should also relax and appreciate the beauty of the city center here at Magsaysay Park. Explore around the city and discover hidden gems such as restaurants, cafes, shops, and all. You could also meet different people and learn more about the city’s rich culture and history!

Aliguay Island

Ah, since you’re already in Dipolog City, you also shouldn’t miss the chance to visit the famous Aliguay Island! It is a very remote location in Zamboanga Del Norte, offering some of the most exciting and relaxing experiences for tourists! Get to witness the pristine and crystal clear waters of a white sand beach where you can also enjoy some water sports activities and feast on fresh seafood caught by the locals!

Sungkilaw Falls

Next up is another favorite tourist destination located at the farthest barangay from the city. But don’t worry! I assure you that it’s definitely going to be worth it. Sungkilaw Falls is a 20-foot high waterfall where tourists love to take a dip and even dive too! The water here is clean and cold, so it’s a perfect summer destination for the locals. There are also tons of exciting activities that tourists love to do here, so be sure to drop by!

Festivals & Events

KATKAT SAKRIPISYO (3003 Steps to Linabo Peak, Dipolog City)

GOOD FRIDAY
A spiritual trekking activity and re-enactment of the death and passion of Jesus Christ which gathers the Catholic community of Zamboanga del Norte and visitors.

DIPOLOG P’GSALABUK FESTIVAL

APRIL – MAY /JULY (Starts on the 3rd week of April)
This festival starts on the 2nd week of May with lively ethnic street dancing, sports, concerts, pageant, cultural shows, etc. A major event includes the annual triathlon, where local and foreign participants race to the finish line in a series of swimming, marathon running and cycling. The entire celebration culminates on July 1st, Adlaw sa Dipolog (Dipolog Day).

FEAST IN HONOR OF ST. VINCENT FERRER

MAY (On the 3rd Saturday of May)
In celebration of the Dipolog City fiesta, locals hold a religious procession around the city’s major thoroughfares that ends at the church patio where devotees dance before the image of St. Vincent Ferrer, our patron saint, to the accompaniment of drum beats.

ADLAW SA DIPOLOG

JUNE – JULY 1 (Starts on the last week of June)
Dipolog City’s founding anniversary with inter- government agency sports activities, awarding and giving recognition to exemplary Dipolognons.

DAHUNOG SA DIPOLOG

SEPT. – OCT. 7 (Starts on the last week of Sept.)
Another festival of Dipolog, Dahunog sa Dipolog is an assemblage of interschool competitions highlighted with a parade band competition and the Miss Teen Tourism Dipolog pageant.. During the event the city streets resonate with the sounds played by the bands specially the drums, thereby, getting the name Dahunog sa Dipolog.

FEAST OF OUR LADY OF THE HOLY ROSARY

October 7
The Marian celebration is celebrated for a month and ends with a Rosary Crusade at the city plaza on the 31st of October in honor of Our Lady of the Rosary.

PASKO SA DIPOLOG (PASADI)

December
In preparation for Christmas, Dipolog holds nightly shows and adorns the streets and plazas with decorations and lights to inspire holiday spirit and unity.
